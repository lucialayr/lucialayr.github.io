Science Some of my current and previous research projects:

Dynamic Vegetation Modelling

LPJ-GUESS is a multiscale dynamical vegetation model, that allows for a detailed representation of vegetation structure. Disturbance, establishment and mortality are represented as stochastic processes.

My work with LPJ-GUESS includes:

  • The role of forest disturbances in modulating climate-driven changes in vegetation composition and land surface properties. I presented some of this work at AGU '23
  • The environmental control of post-disturbance recovery. I presented some of this work at AGU '23
  • The contribution of stochastic processes to model uncertainty. I presented some of this work at EGU '23.

    • alpha-stable stochastic processes

    In applications of dynamical systems theory we usually assume to be in the Gaussian small noise limit. I am interested what happens if this assumption breaks down. I am particularly interested in heavy-tailed data, something we might expect to see more often in climate in the future. I am mainly working with &alpha-stable distributions as the natural extension of the Gaussian case.

  • Our work on early-warning signs of critical transitions is now available as a preprint !
  • I presented an earlier version of this work at the SIAM Conference on Applications of Dynamical Systems '23 .
