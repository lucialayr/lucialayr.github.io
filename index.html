Hello, I'm Lucia

I am a climate scientist and visual artist
currently based at UC Berkeley and TU Munich.

My research employs Global Dynamic Vegetation Modeling
and stochastic dynamical systems to understand
land-surface atmosphere interactions.

My work at TUM is supervised by Prof. Anja Rammig
(Land Surface - Atmosphere Interactions) and Prof. Christian Kuehn
(Multiscale & Stochastic Dynamical Systems).

Scroll for more

The bulk of my work investigates disturbances in the boreal forest and their impact on land surface properties. For this I am using the Dynamic Vegetation Model LPJ-GUESS (Vegetation component of the Earth System Model EC-EARTH). I also worked on more fundamental mathematical problems, most notable alpha-stable processes and their use in modelling climate extremes.

My pre-PhD work focused on climate policy, urban sustainability and water security in Europe and the Middle East. This included work at the Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research, Germany, the Issam Fares Institute for Public Policy at the American University Beirut, Lebanon and the Strategic Foresight team at Evonic Industries.

I am passionate about data visualization and science communication and sometimes work as a scientific illustrator.
